# Moth Luna and the Moonbeam Birdbath

When evening arrived, the jasmine bed changed completely. By day it was a tangle of green stems and shy white stars. By night it became a cloud of fragrance, soft and silver in the moonlight. Luna the moth loved that transformation more than anything.

She was a pale moth with wings like folded petals. Whenever the moon rose, she fluttered out from her leaf shelter to visit the night-blooming flowers and breathe their sweet scent.

One evening a jasmine petal loosened and drifted away before it had fully opened.

Luna saw it spin through the air like a tiny white boat.

"Oh dear," she murmured. "You belong at home."

She followed the petal at once, but the garden was full of silver paths. Moonlight spilled across the stepping stones. Dew gleamed on the grass. A birdbath nearby shone so brightly it looked like a pond made of sky. Everywhere Luna turned, something glimmered.

The petal skimmed low over the thyme, then vanished from sight.

Luna circled once, twice, three times. She began to worry. Each path looked possible. Each pale shimmer might be the right one. Her wings fluttered faster.

Then she caught sight of the birdbath.

Moonlight lay across its water in a broad shining ribbon. For a moment Luna thought she saw the petal drifting there, and she hurried over. But it was only the reflection of a cloud.

"Now I am truly turned around," she said softly.

The night breeze touched her wings.

It smelled of jasmine.

Luna stopped moving.

That scent had followed her from the beginning, but she had been using her eyes so anxiously that she had almost forgotten it. She closed her wings halfway and let the breeze settle around her. It came from the far side of the birdbath, where the jasmine bed curved toward the fence.

She followed the fragrance.

Past the basil pot. Past the smooth stone frog. Past a stem of rosemary that cast a long blue shadow in the moonlight.

There, caught gently in a spiderweb strung between two low branches, was the drifting jasmine petal.

"Found you," Luna whispered.

The web was sticky, but only lightly. Luna hovered close and touched the strand with careful feet until the petal loosened. Then she carried it on her back, flying very slowly so it would not slip again.

When she reached the jasmine bed, she did not try to push the petal back where it had grown. Instead, she tucked it in the crook of two leaves beside the flower it had come from.

"You may rest here," she said.

The flower's fragrance seemed to deepen, as if in thanks.

Luna settled on the edge of the birdbath afterward and looked into the moonbeam water. She could still see a hundred gleams all across the garden. They were lovely, but they no longer confused her.

She knew now that quiet attention did not always come through the eyes. Sometimes the truest path arrived through scent, breeze, and the patient feeling of home.

When she finally flew back to her leaf shelter, the jasmine smell followed her like a soft goodnight.
