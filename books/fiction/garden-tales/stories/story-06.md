# Rabbit Clover and the Pumpkin Moon

At the edge of the garden, where the pumpkin vines sprawled in curling green loops, Clover the rabbit found the roundest pumpkin she had ever seen. It was not the largest pumpkin in the patch, but it was the smoothest and brightest, with skin that shone pale gold in the sunset.

Clover climbed onto it and sat very still.

The pumpkin was warm from the day. From up there she could see the bean poles, the pear tree, and even a sliver of the pond where Willow the turtle liked to watch the sky. Fireflies were beginning to scribble golden loops through the air.

"This is a moon-seat," Clover whispered. "A secret moon-seat just for me."

That evening, she returned with a clover blanket and sat on the pumpkin again while dusk came softly down. It was peaceful. It was perfect. It felt like the sort of place one might keep entirely to oneself.

The next day, however, the garden creatures arrived with news.

"The harvest display will be tonight," said Pip. "We are choosing the best pumpkins to place by the old gate."

"Your round one would be splendid," added Tansy, hovering near a vine blossom.

Clover's paws tightened on the pumpkin stem. "This one?"

"Yes," said Pip.

Clover looked at the smooth golden curve beneath her. She imagined it set by the gate with lanterns around it, admired by everyone. She also imagined losing her secret moon-seat.

"I was saving it," she said.

Nobody scolded her. Tansy only asked, "Saving it for what?"

Clover opened her mouth and then closed it. Saving it, she realized, for sitting alone.

All afternoon she felt torn. She nibble-nibbled a lettuce leaf without tasting it. She hopped around the patch without really seeing the fireflies that would surely come back at dusk. The pumpkin seemed even rounder and dearer the more she thought about giving it up.

When twilight came, she climbed onto the pumpkin once more. The sky above the patch was turning lilac. One by one, the fireflies lit up.

Then she heard music and laughter from near the old gate.

The harvest display had begun.

Clover listened. It sounded warm and happy. Not noisy. Not overwhelming. Just shared.

She looked down at her moon-seat and understood something quiet and important. A lovely thing kept hidden stayed lovely for one. A lovely thing shared could brighten many hearts at once.

So she hopped down, put both paws against the pumpkin, and pushed.

It rolled slowly at first, then steadily along the path. Whenever it wobbled, a pair of fireflies floated ahead, as if guiding a little moon across the garden.

By the time Clover reached the gate, the others had hung leaf lanterns and arranged baskets of herbs and apples. Everyone cheered softly when they saw the round golden pumpkin arrive.

"Clover brought the moon!" Pebble declared.

The pumpkin was placed in the center of the display, where lantern light made it glow. But then Tansy spread a small blanket on top and patted it.

"A place of honor," she said.

"For the display?" Clover asked.

"For anyone who wants to enjoy it."

Soon Moss sat there for a while, then Willow, then even shy Fennel the mouse. Clover discovered that watching friends smile from her pumpkin moon was somehow better than keeping it to herself.

At the end of the evening, after the lanterns were dimmed and the stars had taken over the sky, Clover climbed onto the pumpkin one last time.

Now it no longer felt like a secret seat.

It felt like part of a happy memory shared by the whole garden.

She tucked her paws beneath her and smiled into the night.

Sharing, she decided, had made the moon-seat bigger on the inside.
