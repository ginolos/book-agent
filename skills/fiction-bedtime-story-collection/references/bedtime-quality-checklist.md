# Bedtime Quality Checklist

Use this checklist before compiling the final children's book.

## Story quality
- Every story has a clear beginning, middle, and end.
- The main character name is consistent within the story.
- The title matches the intended naming pattern.
- The emotional arc stays gentle and age-appropriate.
- The ending resolves clearly and reassuringly.

## Collection quality
- No main character names repeat across the 15 stories.
- Core plots are distinct enough from one another.
- Lessons vary while staying complementary.
- The collection feels thematically unified.
- The dedication matches the collection topic and feels warm and specific.
- Introduction and conclusion match the tone of the stories.

## Bedtime suitability
- Tone stays calm, warm, and safe.
- Conflict is present but not distressing.
- Language is vivid but easy to follow for ages 4-8.
- Frightening or harsh material has been softened or removed.
- Stories end in a restful emotional place.

## Final output
- The compiled markdown includes title, subtitle, introduction, all 15 stories, and conclusion.
- Story order feels balanced.
- The manuscript is ready for pandoc conversion.
