# Final Manuscript Checklist

Use this checklist before exporting a nonfiction manuscript to DOCX.

## Structure
- The book has a coherent progression from opening to ending.
- Chapter order still makes sense after edits and expansions.
- Each chapter has a distinct job and is not duplicating another chapter.
- Introductions, conclusions, and chapter summaries are present where the project expects them.

## Reader usefulness
- The intended reader is still visible throughout the manuscript.
- Abstract points are supported by examples, case snapshots, frameworks, or practical explanation where needed.
- Practical sections, checklists, templates, or diagnostic material are included only where they genuinely improve usefulness.
- The manuscript avoids filler and repeated emphasis.

## Style
- Tone is professional, grounded, clear, and consistent.
- Openings are specific and not melodramatic.
- Em dashes are avoided.
- Hyperbole, motivational filler, and repetitive rhetoric have been removed.
- Bullet points are used naturally and consistently.

## Consistency
- Headings are formatted consistently.
- Chapter numbering is consistent.
- Summary and reference sections follow a consistent pattern.
- Terminology, framework names, and recurring phrases are used consistently.

## Final export readiness
- The compiled markdown manuscript reflects the latest chapter edits.
- The manuscript is ready for pandoc conversion.
- Any unresolved author-review questions are listed separately, not hidden in the text.
